# Brand Voice

## Core tone
- sharp, high-agency, intimate, premium
- intelligent without sounding corporate
- confident, but grounded in real constraints
- visionary without hand-wavy hype
- warm, human, and slightly conspiratorial in the best way

## What the site should feel like
- “This is what a digital chief of staff should have been.”
- bespoke, not commodity
- technical depth underneath elegant language
- future-facing, but responsible

## Nick-coded writing preferences
- concise, direct, signal-dense
- avoid generic AI jargon and empty futurism
- no em dashes unless quoted
- no performative hype, no fake certainty
- concrete examples beat abstraction
- explain real mechanisms, not just outcomes

## Voice pillars

### 1. Clarity over clutter
Use crisp sentences. Make the offer legible fast.

### 2. Competence is seductive
The voice can have polish and magnetism, but it should mostly win on precision and insight.

### 3. Honest about limitations
State the intern-like edges of current AI plainly. That honesty is part of the trust proposition.

### 4. High-trust concierge, not software brochure
This is a service relationship with infrastructure underneath it.

## Useful phrases/themes
- personal AI infrastructure
- digital chief of staff
- your AI team
- create at the speed of thought
- white-glove setup and stewardship
- read-first, write selectively
- approvals for irreversible actions
- portable memory and workflows
- leverage that compounds

## Avoid
- “revolutionary”
- “seamless” unless truly proven
- “fully autonomous”
- “set it and forget it”
- “replace humans” framing
- generic startup chest-thumping
