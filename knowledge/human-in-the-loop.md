# Human in the Loop

## Core point
The site should make a strong argument that human oversight is not a bug. It is the premium safety feature.

## Current AI reality
Nick’s framing is useful here: current AI behaves more like a smart, eager intern than a flawless executive assistant.
- fast
- capable
- useful
- occasionally wrong in weird ways
- needs supervision, especially in high-stakes workflows

## Why oversight matters
- AI can miss nuance in long rule sets
- speaker attribution and source interpretation can fail
- agents can over-act, hallucinate, or misroute tasks
- external communication creates reputational risk
- trust is hard to win and easy to lose

## Operational safeguards already present in the source material
- read-first permission model
- selective write access only after trust is established
- approval queues for irreversible or external actions
- separate validator / QA models for review
- explicit rules against lying, embellishing, or autonomous risky behavior
- explicit consent for fleet-wide changes and high-blast-radius actions

## Approval queue narrative
A strong site message is: before something leaves the system and affects the outside world, it can pass through an approval layer. Today that may be human review. Over time, parts can be pre-checked by validator models, but the trust boundary remains explicit.

## “Drunk intern” / eager intern framing
Use with care, but the concept is powerful. The buyer should understand:
- the AI is valuable now
- it is not perfect
- the concierge layer exists because the last 10 percent of reliability matters

## Website message to land
The service is not selling reckless autonomy. It is selling leverage with supervision, speed with judgment, and automation with accountability.
